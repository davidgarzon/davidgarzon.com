# Operating Principles

## Systems over features

I prioritise building coherent systems that scale and compound over shipping isolated features. A well-designed system creates leverage; individual features without a clear mental model create technical and organisational debt.

## Compounding over heroics

Sustainable impact comes from habits, processes, and incremental improvements that compound over time. Hero moments feel good but rarely change outcomes as much as consistent execution and long-term thinking.

## Measurement as a habit

What gets measured gets improved—but only if measurement is built into the flow of work, not treated as a one-off exercise. I embed analytics, experimentation, and review loops into how teams operate.

## AI as a collaborator, not a tool

I treat AI as a thinking partner and amplifier of judgment, not just a productivity tool. The goal is to augment decision-making and creativity while keeping human judgment in the loop.

## Execution velocity with strategic patience

I value speed in execution—sprinting, shipping, iterating—while maintaining patience on strategy. Short-term urgency on delivery; long-term discipline on vision and bets.

## Data-driven but judgment-informed

Data informs decisions, but it doesn't replace judgment. I use metrics and experiments to reduce uncertainty, then apply experience and intuition to make the final call.

## Clarity before consensus

I prefer clear ownership, explicit trade-offs, and documented decisions over endless alignment meetings. Clarity enables action; consensus can slow it down when taken too far.

## Ship to learn

The best way to validate an idea is to put it in front of users. I favour small, fast experiments over big, late launches. Shipping is a learning mechanism, not just a delivery event.
