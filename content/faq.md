# FAQ

## What's your product philosophy?

I believe products should solve real problems in a way that compounds over time. I focus on systems rather than isolated features: building coherent mental models, clear decision frameworks, and habits that scale. Good product work is about reducing uncertainty through structure, then shipping quickly to learn.

## How do you approach AI in products?

I treat AI as a collaborator that augments human judgment, not a replacement for it. In products, this means designing for AI-native experiences—where the AI is core to the value proposition—rather than retrofitting AI onto existing flows. I care about orchestration, decision support, and making complex information actionable.

## What industries have you worked in?

I have experience across fintech, healthtech, SaaS, marketplaces, and developer tools. I gravitate toward domains with meaningful complexity and where product decisions directly drive business outcomes.

## How do you run product discovery?

I run discovery as a continuous loop: framing the problem, forming hypotheses, designing lightweight experiments, and synthesising learnings. I favour structured approaches—decision frameworks, experiment tracking, clear success metrics—so that discoveries actually influence roadmap decisions rather than gathering dust.

## What's your leadership style?

I aim for clarity over consensus: clear ownership, explicit trade-offs, and documented decisions. I lead by building trust through consistency, by enabling teams with frameworks and context, and by staying close enough to the work to make good calls without micromanaging.

## How do you think about metrics?

Metrics should be tied to outcomes, not vanity. I focus on a small set of leading and lagging indicators that connect to business and user value. I also treat measurement as a habit—embedded in processes—so the team can learn and adapt without big, one-off analysis projects.

## What tools do you use?

I use a mix of tools for product, analytics, and collaboration: Notion/Confluence for docs, Linear/Jira for roadmap and execution, analytics platforms for experimentation, and LLMs for drafting, synthesis, and decision support. The exact stack depends on the team; I care more about discipline than any single tool.

## How many direct reports have you led?
Up to 15 direct reports. Total org footprint: ~96 people.
